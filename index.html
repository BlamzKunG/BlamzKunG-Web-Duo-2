About Thai Desserts

Thai desserts, or "Khanom Thai", are an important part of Thai culture and celebrations. They are known for their vibrant colors, unique textures, and delicate flavors, often made with coconut, rice, tropical fruits, and fragrant flowers. Thai sweets are not just food—they are a symbol of hospitality, tradition, and artistry.

Popular Thai Desserts

Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niew Mamuang)

Sweet sticky rice topped with ripe mango and coconut milk. A beloved summer treat!

Khanom Buang

Thai crispy pancakes filled with sweet or savory toppings, often with coconut cream and golden egg yolk threads.

Khanom Chan

Layered jelly-like dessert made from rice flour, coconut milk, and pandan, known for its beautiful colors and soft texture.

Thong Yip

Golden flower-shaped sweets made from egg yolks and syrup, symbolizing wealth and good fortune.

Luk Chup

Miniature fruit-shaped sweets made from mung bean paste, dipped in colorful jelly. Beautiful and delicious!

Simple Recipes

Mango Sticky Rice

  • 1 cup sticky rice
  • 1 ripe mango
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp sugar, pinch of salt
  1. Cook sticky rice until soft.
  2. Mix coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat gently.
  3. Pour coconut milk over rice. Serve with sliced mango.

Khanom Buang

  • Rice flour crepes
  • Coconut cream
  • Egg yolk threads (Foi Thong)
  1. Make thin crepes on a pan.
  2. Top with coconut cream and egg yolk threads.
  3. Fold and serve crispy.

Khanom Chan

  • Rice flour, tapioca flour
  • Coconut milk
  • Pandan juice
  1. Mix flours, coconut milk, and pandan.
  2. Pour thin layer in mold, steam, repeat for layers.
  3. Cool and cut into pieces.
